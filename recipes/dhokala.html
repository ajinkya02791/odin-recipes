Dhokla is a steamed savory cake made from fermented batter derived from rice and chickpea flour (besan). Originating from the western Indian state of Gujarat, dhokla is light, fluffy, and slightly tangy with a hint of sweetness, making it a popular breakfast item or tea-time snack. It is healthy, protein-rich, and typically served with green chutney or sweet tamarind chutney.

Ingridents

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1 tbsp semolina (sooji/rava) optional, for texture
  • 1 tsp citric acid or 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 3/4 cup water (adjust to get a smooth batter)
  • 1 tsp Eno fruit salt (or 1/2 tsp baking soda + 1/2 tsp lemon juice)
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • A few curry leaves
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds

Directions

Step 1 :

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine besan, semolina (if using), turmeric, sugar, salt, and citric acid or lemon juice.
  2. Gradually add water and whisk to form a smooth, lump-free batter. It should be slightly thick but pourable.
  3. Let the batter rest for 10 to 15 minutes.

Step 2 :

  1. Meanwhile, prepare a steamer or heat water in a large pan with a trivet.
  2. Grease a round dhokla plate or any flat-bottomed container.
  3. Just before steaming, add Eno fruit salt to the batter and mix gently. The batter will become frothy and rise.
  4. Immediately pour it into the greased plate and place it in the steamer.
  5. Cover and steam for 15 to 20 minutes on medium heat, or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  6. Remove and let it cool slightly before cutting into squares.

Step 3 :

  1. Heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and let them crackle.
  2. Add sesame seeds, green chilies, and curry leaves. Saut√© for a few seconds.
  3. Add water, sugar, and lemon juice. Mix and simmer for a minute.
  4. Pour the tempering evenly over the cut dhokla.
