Khichdi is a classic Indian comfort food made from rice and lentils, gently spiced and often cooked with vegetables. It's simple, nutritious, easy to digest, and widely considered a healing or “sattvic” dish, often eaten when one is unwell or in need of a light, wholesome meal. Depending on the region, khichdi can range from plain to richly spiced, soft or fluffy, and is often served with curd, papad, or pickle.

Ingridients :

  • 1/2 cup rice (any variety, usually basmati or short grain)
  • 1/2 cup split yellow moong dal (or toor dal)
  • 1 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water (for soft, porridge-like khichdi; use 3 cups for drier version)
  • 1/4 cup green peas
  • 1/4 cup chopped carrots
  • 1/4 cup chopped potatoes
  • 1 tomato, chopped

Steps :

Step 1 :

  1. Rinse the rice and dal together 2 to 3 times in water. Soak them for 15 to 30 minutes (optional but helps with even cooking).
  2. Drain and set aside.

Step 2 :

    Using a Pressure Cooker :

  1. Heat ghee/oil in the cooker. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add asafoetida and turmeric.
  3. (Optional) Add vegetables and sauté for 2 to 3 minutes.
  4. Add soaked rice and dal. Mix everything gently.
  5. Add 4 cups of water and salt to taste.
  6. Close the lid and cook for 2 to 3 whistles on medium heat.
  7. Let the pressure release naturally. Open the lid and gently stir. Add more hot water if needed to adjust consistency.
